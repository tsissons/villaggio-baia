Welcome to Calabria

If a Vespa-riding, siesta-loving chaotically unadorned version of Italy still exists, it's probably here in Calabria, the 'toe' of the boot that kicks Sicily into the Tyrrhenian Sea. Scarred by recurrent earthquakes and lacking a Matera or Lecce to give it high-flying tourist status, this is a land of throwbacks and traditions, sheltered by craggy mountains. If you're intent on seeing a candid and uncensored version of la dolce vita that hasn't been dressed up for tourist consumption, look no further.

It's hard to resist the allure of its attractive Tyrrhenian coastline dotted with picturesque towns and villages like Tropea and Scilla. The mountainous centre is dominated by three national parks, none of them well-explored. Easily the region's biggest snare are its Greek artefacts collected from ruins, archaeological sites, and ancient shipwrecks - catalogued in some truly impressive museums.
